# Module 1: Foundation

## 1.1 What is Price Action?

Price action is the movement of price over time. Instead of using indicators, we read the **raw price** on the chart to understand what buyers and sellers are doing.

**Key Concept:** Every candle tells a story of the battle between buyers and sellers.

## 1.3 Swing Points

A **swing high** is a peak where price reversed down.
A **swing low** is a trough where price reversed up.

### Identifying Swing Points

**Swing High:** A candle with lower highs on BOTH sides
```

```

**Swing Low:** A candle with higher lows on BOTH sides

### Why Swing Points Matter

1. They define market structure (HH, HL, LH, LL)
2. Liquidity sits above swing highs and below swing lows
3. Break of a swing point = Break of Structure (BOS)

# Module 2: Liquidity

## 2.1 What is Liquidity?

**Liquidity = Stop losses clustered at a price level**

When you place a trade, your stop loss becomes "liquidity" for bigger players. Institutions need liquidity to fill their large orders.

### The Game

- Retail traders place stops at obvious levels
- Institutions push price INTO those stops
- Stops get triggered = orders filled
- Price reverses

**You want to trade WITH institutions, not against them.**

# Module 5: Premium & Discount

## 5.1 The Concept

Institutions buy low, sell high. Simple.

- **Discount** = Below 50% of range = BUY zone
- **Premium** = Above 50% of range = SELL zone

### Visualized

---

## 5.2 How to Use It

### For Longs

1. Wait for price to enter DISCOUNT (below 50%)
2. Look for bullish BOS
3. Enter on FVG or order block
4. Target: Premium zone or liquidity above

### For Shorts

1. Wait for price to enter PREMIUM (above 50%)
2. Look for bearish BOS
3. Enter on FVG or order block
4. Target: Discount zone or liquidity below

### The Rule

> "Buy in discount, sell in premium"
> 
> Don't buy at highs. Don't sell at lows.

# Module 6: Time

## 6.1 Killzones

Not all hours are equal. Institutions are most active during specific windows.

### Key Sessions (ET)

| Session | Time (ET) | Character |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Asia | 8 PM - 12 AM | Consolidation, builds liquidity |
| London | 3 AM - 6 AM | First major moves, sweeps Asia |
| NY Open | 9:30 AM - 11 AM | Highest volume, real moves |
| NY Lunch | 12 PM - 2 PM | Chop, avoid trading |
| NY Close | 2 PM - 4 PM | Second chance, continuation |

### Best Times to Trade

**Primary:** 9:30 AM - 11:00 AM ET (NY Open)
**Secondary:** 2:00 PM - 3:30 PM ET (NY Afternoon)

### Times to Avoid

- 12:00 PM - 2:00 PM ET (Lunch chop)
- Major news events (unless you trade news)
- Low volume pre-market

---

## 6.2 Session Liquidity

Each session builds liquidity for the next.

### The Pattern

```
Asia builds range â†’ London sweeps one side â†’ NY continues or reverses
```

**Common Scenario:**
1. Asia creates equal highs/lows

3. NY rallies (real move)

### Using This

Before NY Open, ask:
- What did Asia build? (Range? Trend?)
- What did London sweep? (Highs? Lows?)
- Where is liquidity now?

# Module 7: HTF Bias

## 7.1 Why HTF Bias Matters

Your **Higher Timeframe (HTF) bias** is your compass. It tells you which direction to trade.

**Rule:** Only take LTF trades that align with HTF bias.

---

## 7.2 Determining HTF Bias

### Step 1: Check Daily/4H Structure

- Higher Highs + Higher Lows = **Bullish**
- Lower Highs + Lower Lows = **Bearish**

### Step 2: Premium or Discount?

- Price in premium (above 50%) = Look for sells
- Price in discount (below 50%) = Look for buys

### Step 3: Where is Liquidity?

- Untaken highs above = Bullish target
- Untaken lows below = Bearish target

### Step 4: Session Context

- London swept lows = NY likely bullish
- London swept highs = NY likely bearish

---

## 7.3 HTF Bias Checklist

Before every trade:

| Question | Bullish | Bearish |
|----------|---------|---------|
| Daily structure? | HH/HL | LH/LL |
| Premium or discount? | Discount | Premium |
| Nearest liquidity? | Highs above | Lows below |
| Session sweep? | Lows taken | Highs taken |

**3-4 align = Strong bias = Trade with confidence**
**Mixed = No trade or reduced size**

# Module 9: Risk Management

## 9.1 The Only Edge That Matters

You can have the best strategy, but without risk management, you WILL blow up.

### Core Rules

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| Risk 1-2% per trade | Survive losing streaks |
| Max 2 trades per day | Prevent overtrading |
| Stop after 2 losses | Protect your psychology |
| Always use stop loss | Define your risk |

---

# Module 10: Psychology

## 10.1 The Mental Game

Trading is 80% psychology, 20% strategy.

### Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Revenge trading | Walk away after 2 losses |
| FOMO entries | Only trade your setup |
| Moving stop loss | Set it and forget it |
| Overtrading | Quality over quantity |
| No journal | Review every trade |

---

## 10.2 The Mindset

**Process > Outcome**

A losing trade can be a good trade (if you followed your rules).
A winning trade can be a bad trade (if you broke your rules).

**Focus on execution, not results.**

---

## 10.3 Daily Routine

### Pre-Market (30 min before open)
1. Check Daily/4H structure
2. Identify key levels (liquidity, FVGs)
3. Determine HTF bias
4. Note any news events

### During Session
1. Wait for setup (don't force)
2. Execute with discipline
3. Manage trade (trail stop if needed)
4. Stop after daily goal or 2 losses

### Post-Market (15 min)
1. Screenshot trades
2. Journal: What worked? What didn't?
3. Review emotions: Were you disciplined?
